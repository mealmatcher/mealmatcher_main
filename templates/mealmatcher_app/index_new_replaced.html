Meet, Eat, Repeat

A (visionary) service that lets you sign up to share a meal with someone new. MealMatcher brings together Princeton students to share meals in any of the six dining halls and at all three mealtimes. You can choose to either create your own time or select to join an open meal. We will email you when you match with someone or if someone cancels. My Meals shows you all the meals you have signed up for and even lets you cancel meals or edit what you'll be wearing. When it's time for your meal, show up at the card swipe of the dining hall and meet your meal buddy!

Find a Meal

Enter where and when you'd like to eat, as well as what you will be wearing, and we will match you with someone!

Open Meals

Select a meal that someone has already signed up for to join them.

My Meals

View, edit, and delete the meals you have signed up for.

Frequently Asked Questions


What is the purpose of this website?
MealMatcher is intended to be a service for Princeton students to randomly match with each other. Meals are always 1-on-1 and can be in any of the campus dining halls. It is meant to help students quickly find someone else to eat with if they'd rather not eat alone and to provide a platform to meet new people over good food and conversation.

When will the website be launched?
MealMatcher is currently not fully launched or supported and usable only for testing purposes. Our team will be launching it campus-wide in Fall 2015.

How do I get started and sign up for a meal?
Log in with your Princeton NetID and password and select Find a Meal. Either fill out the form on the left to create a custom meal or choose to join an Open Meal using the calendar on the right. You will be given an email notification when your meal has been matched, and also an email remainder an hour before the meal. You can also view/edit meals in the My Meals page.

Why am I not getting my notification emails?
Your emails are likely being sent to your spam folder. Please look for them there and safelist our email address.

How will I find the person I matched with?
At the time of the meal, please show up in front of the cardswipe of the dining hall of the meal and look for your match based on the attire listed in the remainder email. Your person should be looking for you too!

Why do I have to list my attire?
Because meals are intended to be entirely anonymous before meeting your match, we do not provide any other identifying information to find your meal buddy, so please make your attire as descriptive as possible. Note that you can change your attire up to an hour before the meal, so don't worry if you don't know what you'll be wearing yet.

What happens if I sign up for a meal and cannot make it?
Don't worry! You can cancel a matched meal up to an hour beforehand and your match will receive an email notification about the cancellation. However, within an hour of the meal it becomes locked in and you cannot cancel it at that point, so make sure to make it to your meal!

My meal was matched but when I went to cardswipe at the designated time my person wasn't there! What happened?
Your person may have forgotten about to meal. We're sorry this happened, and you can report the person not showing up using the feedback form for Expired Meals under the My Meals page. We will flag the user and temporarily suspend them from using the website if they keep missing meals.

I don't like listing attire for finding my meal match. Isn't there a better way?
Our team is aware of the issues with attire being the only way to find your match and are actively working on improvements to this system. There will likely be a change before the launch. If you have any suggestions or comments, feel free to email us at princeton.meal.matcher@gmail.com

Who made this site?
Our team consists of Kevin Wang, Drew Wallace, Andreas Dias, Valerie Morin, and Andrew Charette.
